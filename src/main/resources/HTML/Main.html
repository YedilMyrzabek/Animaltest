About Astana

Astana, the capital of Kazakhstan, is a vibrant city known for its unique architecture and rich history. The city became the capital in 1997 and has since grown into a symbol of Kazakhstan’s future.

Brief History

Originally founded as Akmolinsk in 1830, the city has gone through several name changes. It was known as Tselinograd during the Soviet era, then Akmola, before finally becoming Astana and now Nur-Sultan in honor of Kazakhstan's first president, Nursultan Nazarbayev.
