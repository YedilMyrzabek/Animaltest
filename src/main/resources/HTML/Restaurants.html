Local Cuisine

Astana offers a variety of traditional Kazakh dishes, including besbarmak, kazy, and baursak. These dishes reflect the nomadic heritage of the Kazakh people.

Beshbarmak: The Quintessential Kazakh Dish

Beshbarmak, a traditional Kazakh dish, is more than just a meal; it is a symbol of hospitality and cultural heritage. The name "Beshbarmak" translates to "five fingers" in Kazakh, reflecting the traditional way of eating the dish with one’s hands. This culinary delight is often prepared for special occasions, celebrations, and family gatherings, embodying the essence of Kazakh culture and tradition.

Ingredients and Preparation

The primary ingredients of Beshbarmak include meat (commonly lamb or beef), onions, salt, pepper, and spices. Additionally, homemade or store-bought dough is used to create the pasta-like noodles that accompany the dish.

The preparation of Beshbarmak begins with cooking the meat. It is simmered in salted water until tender, and various spices such as bay leaves and black pepper are added to enhance the flavor. Once the meat is fully cooked, it is either shredded into small pieces or cut into chunks, ready to be served.


Bauyrsak: The Heart of Kazakh Cuisine

Bauyrsak, a beloved traditional Kazakh pastry, is more than just a snack; it embodies the essence of Kazakh hospitality and cultural tradition. Often described as "fried dough," Bauyrsak is cherished for its soft, airy texture and its role in celebrating special occasions and gatherings.

Ingredients and Preparation

The preparation of Bauyrsak begins with a simple yet flavorful dough. The primary ingredients include flour, yeast, sugar, salt, milk, and butter. These ingredients are combined to form a soft, elastic dough that is allowed to rise. The dough’s richness comes from the butter, which adds a delightful flavor and tenderness to the pastry.

After the dough has risen, it is divided into small pieces and shaped into round or oval forms. These pieces are then deep-fried in hot oil until they turn golden brown. The frying process is crucial, as it gives Bauyrsak its signature crispy exterior while keeping the inside soft and fluffy.


Kymyz: The Traditional Kazakh Fermented Mare's Milk

Kymyz, a traditional Kazakh beverage, holds a special place in the culinary and cultural landscape of Kazakhstan. Made from fermented mare's milk, Kymyz is not just a drink but a symbol of Kazakh heritage, embodying the rich traditions and practices of the nomadic lifestyle.

Ingredients and Preparation

The primary ingredient in Kymyz is mare's milk, which is prized for its unique properties and flavor. To prepare Kymyz, fresh mare's milk is first collected and then fermented using naturally occurring lactic acid bacteria and yeast. This fermentation process can take several days, during which the milk transforms into a slightly sour, effervescent beverage.

The fermentation process is critical in developing the distinctive taste and probiotic benefits of Kymyz. During fermentation, the milk undergoes a transformation, developing a tangy flavor and a mildly carbonated texture. The resulting drink has a slightly sour taste with a refreshing quality, making it a popular choice among those seeking a traditional and healthful beverage.
